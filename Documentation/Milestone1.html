Milestone 1 Leader:

Chosen Project: Wedgewood Pacific Employee Database (Old Project)

The Wedgewood Pacific Corporation (WPC), founded in 1957 in Seattle, Washington, has grown into an internationally recognized organization. The company is located in two buildings. One building houses the Administration, Accounting, Finance, and Human Resources departments, and the second houses the Production, Marketing, and Information Systems departments. The company database contains data about employees; departments; projects; assets, such as computer equipment; and other aspects of company operations.

Business Objectives (Old Project)

• To create a database to track employees, departments, projects, and assignments.

• WP Corp. wants to be able to access and manipulate data about employees, departments, projects, and assignments.

• WP Corp. wants to keep track of the number of hours worked by each employee assigned to any given project.

• Be able to keep track of hours in 1/2 hour increments (ex. 44.0, 22.5).

Unifying Theme: (New Project)

Databases are a collection of raw data which is given meaning, the collection of meaningful data becomes valuable information which can be utilized for present and future applications in the form of human knowledge. Information system's role is to use this information most efficiently by supporting the critical aspects of running an organization, namely with communication, record-keeping, and data analysis by providing more efficient and accessible methods of collecting, transferring, and manipulating data with the heavy use of automated technology. These method's ideologies are established under the study of information architectures that involved solving the fundamental problems of accessing and using the vast amounts of information available to a company.

Given that information architecture establishes the best methods of dealing with the information, the role of information systems utilizes those methods within the technological system, all while dealing with the interpretation of data; I.T. Policy and Law directly affects how the data must be handled within those categories by emphasizing the importance of encrypting sensitive data, protecting web form inputs from SQL injection, and general practices for physical and network security.
